The Competition

The SpaceX Hyperloop competition was conceived to accelerate the development of a functional hyperloop system through the effort of various university-based and independent engineering teams. When the competition kicked off in October of 2015, over 1,200 teams had entered. Following a recent evaluation and selection process, SpaceX has invited 120 university teams to either further develop a single subsystem or fabricate a prototype of their transportation pod. We are thrilled to announce that upon review of our preliminary pod design, SpaceX had extended our team an invitation to participate further in the competition. In January of 2016 , we had the opportunity to present our final design package to SpaceX, potential sponsors and authorities in the transportation industry at the SpaceX Hyperloop Design Weekend hosted by Texas A&M University.

Our Mission

As the preliminary competition had come to a close, the Drexel University Hyperloop Team was chosen to be among some of the few teams selected to further their design and prepare for 3/4 scale testing on a mile long hyperloop test track. Although there has been much success in the way of concept formation and initial design many challenges still await us as we strive to build a test model of our design. Since June 2015, we have brought the dream of innovative transportation technology and hands-on experience to students on campus and around the world. Remaining at the forefront of hyperloop pod technology since its conception, we use a collaborative approach to actuate our project goals. Since the initial competition, our team has been growing into a fully functional business model that we hope will inspire students at Drexel University and abroad to pursue their dreams and work hard to not only do well but to change the world and have fun while doing so.
